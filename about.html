Prof. Ngozi Chuma Udeh

Commissioner of Education, Anambra state,
Nigeria

About
Background

Ngozi has received National and State Awards and Recognition as an outstanding Literary Artist. Ngozi is a girl-child initiative activist and proponent of zero illiteracy for Nigerian women. She has carried out extensive researches on development, empowerment, and protection of women and girl children, especially in war situations. She is also a strong apostle of Arts for the society’s sake and she is of the belief that arts and literary dialogues are the panacea for remedying the world’s chaotic experiences. She has Thirty (30) book publications (counting Novels, Children Fictions, Textbooks, Collection of Poems and Dramas). She has to her academic credit ninety five (95) publications in Abstracted Journals. She has attended over sixty five(65) Conferences (Local and International) with sixty (60) papersdelivered both internationally and locally. As an outstanding public speaker, she has delivered about twelve Keynotes in both National and International Conferences. She has been specially invited to about twelve States in the Federal Republic of Nigeria to deliver academic lectures in Annual Literary Colloquiums. She has visited forty-five Universities outside the country in her academic quest. In 2013 and on a special invitation, she attended and delivered a lecture at a symposium Celebrating Jorge Luis Borges, held at the University of Bueno Aires, in Argentina. She is an External Examiner to National College, Tamilnadu India and Bharathidasan University, PalkalaiPerur. She was appointed the Fiction Editor, Sentinel Literary Quarterly Journals London, United Kingdom in 2012, and Editor/Reviewer PMLA (Journal of the Modern Language Association MLA in 2014. Chuma-Udeh, Ngozi’s Trends and Issues in NigerianLiterature was cited as a Main line Text book at the library of University of Leeds, London in 2011 and a Recognition and Documentation of Authorship by the Library of Congress Virtual International Authority File: Teachers on Strike (VIAF ID: 187463577) was given in 2012. Ngozi belongs to numerous Academic Associations and Literary bodies prominent among which are; Modern Language Association (MLA), African Literature Association (ALA), Pan-African Writers Association, Nigerian Association for Educational Administration and Planning (NAEAP), Association of International Education Administrators (AIEA), The Literati Philosophia and Association of Nigerian Authors (ANA). She presently holds the dual functions of the Dean of Arts and the University Orator, Chukwuemeka Odumegwu Ojukwu University (formerly Anambra State University). She is married and blessed with children Her dream projects are: “Bringing back the book culture for life and for posterity,” and “Getting Girl-Children off the Street.”

Education

Ngozi Chuma-Udeh is an Educationist and a Professor of English. She is an orator, a novelist, a poet and a dramatist. She holds a double Masters Degrees, (Med) Educational Administration, 1997) and (MA English 2004). She also holds two Doctorate Degrees, PhD Educational Administration (2002), PhD English { Comparative Literature stress } (2010). Ngozi served as the Head, Department of English, Anambra State University for two tenures, Coordinator General English Studies for two tenures, Associate Dean of Arts for two tenures, Dean Faculty of Arts for three academic sessions and Chair, Local Organizing Committee for Faculty of Arts International Conferences for five years. She has supervised postgraduate students (MA, PhD) both in her local University and Internationally. She is an International External Examiner both in Educational Administration and in English Studies. She has been deeply involved in various curriculum planning in her local University and in the Educational Sector of her home State. She has participated in the development of Education programmes and has helped in managing, implementing, monitoring, evaluating and reporting the progress of education programmes in the Anambra State Post Primary School Board as a Principal Officer/ Supervisor. She has designed and implemented strategies for improving learning outcomes for children in the rural areas in her home State. Twice an International Award Winner, her first novel Teachers on Strikewon the West African Literary Award in 2005 and her second novelEchoes of a New Dawnwon the Association of African Women Writers (AAWW) Masterpiece Award in Senegal (2010).
