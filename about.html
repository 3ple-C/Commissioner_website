Prof. Ngozi Chuma Udeh

Commissioner of Education, Anambra state,
Nigeria

About

Ngozi Chuma-Udeh is an Educationist and a Professor of English. She is an orator, a novelist, a poet and a dramatist. She holds a double Masters Degrees, (Med) Educational Administration, 1997) and (MA English 2004). She also holds two Doctorate Degrees, PhD Educational Administration (2002), PhD English { Comparative Literature stress } (2010). Ngozi served as the Head, Department of English, Anambra State University for two tenures, Coordinator General English Studies for two tenures, Associate Dean of Arts for two tenures, Dean Faculty of Arts for three academic sessions and Chair, Local Organizing Committee for Faculty of Arts International Conferences for five years. She has supervised postgraduate students (MA, PhD) both in her local University and Internationally. She is an International External Examiner both in Educational Administration and in English Studies. She has been deeply involved in various curriculum planning in her local University and in the Educational Sector of her home State. She has participated in the development of Education programmes and has helped in managing, implementing, monitoring, evaluating and reporting the progress of education programmes in the Anambra State Post Primary School Board as a Principal Officer/ Supervisor. She has designed and implemented strategies for improving learning, learning outcomes for children in the rural areas in her home State. Twice an International Award Winner, her first novel Teachers on Strikewon the West African Literary Award in 2005 and her second novelEchoes of a New Dawnwon the Association of African Women Writers (AAWW) Masterpiece Award in Senegal (2010).
